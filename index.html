Welcome, I'm Julia Moscrop — a Psychotherapist based in North Leeds.

I support children, young people, adults and families through relational, trauma-informed therapy. With over twenty years' experience across therapeutic, educational, and community settings, I offer a calm, safe, and compassionate space for emotional expression, healing, and growth.

My practice integrates creative and embodied approaches, grounded in contemporary psychotherapy, attachment theory, and developmental understanding.

I bring insight into trauma, neurodiversity, attachment challenges, and the complexities of family life. I can also work collaboratively with parents and carers to strengthen connection, resilience, and understanding.

I'm passionate about creating spaces where people feel deeply heard, understood, and safe enough to explore their inner world.

My path into psychotherapy began over twenty years ago through youth and community work. I've worked in a wide range of roles — from youth offending and race equality projects to playwork, international youth work, and youth theatre. These early experiences taught me how to meet people where they are, with curiosity, respect, and an open mind.

Alongside my community work I trained as a counsellor on a BACP-accredited Postgraduate Diploma and recently completed a UKCP-accredited MSc and Diploma in Child Psychotherapy. Over the years I've supported children, adolescents, and adults in school settings, women's therapy services, and private practice.
